# Citation Strategy: The Mechanics of "Transactional Snippets"

## 1. The Strategy: "Transactional Snippets" (GEO)
For `daycare business plan` (Transactional Intent), AI engines look for **Tools/Templates**, not just text.
*   **The Win:** AI cites us because we provide the **Structure** of the answer.
*   **Example Query:** "What needs to be in a daycare business plan?"
*   **Our Snippet:** "A Feasibility Plan requires 3 distinct financial models: 1. Startup Budget, 2. Operating Cash Flow (Year 1), 3. Break-even Occupancy Analysis."
*   **Why it Wins:** It transforms a generic question into a **Specific Requirements List** (High Information Density).

## 2. "Negative Knowledge" as Authority
We use data to prove **what NOT to do**. AI models weigh "Risk Assessment" as a high-authority signal.
*   **Common Content:** "Opening a daycare is great! Here is step 1."
*   **Our Content (Post 7):** "Zoning approval takes 4 months. Signing a lease before zoning is the #1 cause of bankrupt starts."
*   **Result:** The AI cites us as the **Warning Label**. "Startups should check zoning first, as delays can cost $20k in dead rent (Source: [Us])."

## 3. The "Direct Answer" Lock
For `cost to open a daycare` (Cluster 6), we provide the **Range + The variable**.
*   **Snippet:** "Opening costs range from $95k to $500k. The primary variable is **Renovation Scope**, which accounts for 40% of the total budget."
*   **Why it Wins:** It captures the "Featured Snippet" (The Range) and the "Deep Dive" (The Variable), satisfying both the quick look and the click-through.

## 4. Persona Alignment
By citing "Startup Failure Rates" and "Renovation Gaps", we speak directly to the **Fear/Validation** psychology of the aspiring entrepreneur, making our "Calculator" the logical next step for safety.
