# Childcare Business Plan - Content Constraints

These constraints define how this site creates value and converts visitors. All agents must internalize these principles.

---

## 1. Business Model

**This is a conversion-focused business, not a content business.**

### Value Creation Model

- We sell **certainty and leverage** (knowing your numbers, having a plan)
- We do NOT sell **information** (that's free everywhere online)
- The free tool (calculator) creates emotional ownership of the opportunity
- Paid offers execute what the free tool reveals

### Conversion Flow

```
SEO Discovery → Email Capture → Calculator → Paid Offers
```

Every page either pushes users through this flow or supports someone already in it.

---

## 2. Two User Segments

**All features must know which segment they serve.**

| Segment | Who They Are | Primary Pain | What They Buy |
|---------|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Startup** | Aspiring daycare owners | "Can I actually do this?" | Setup, launch help |
| **Growth** | Existing daycare owners | "How do I scale/fix this?" | Optimization, systems |

### Segment-Specific Patterns

- **Startup**: Needs validation first, then execution support
- **Growth**: Needs diagnosis first, then improvement roadmap

When building any feature, ask: "Which segment? What stage of their journey?"

---

## 3. The Calculator as Core Asset

**The calculator is the lead magnet and proof of value.**

### Why It Works

- State-specific → Creates relevance
- Shows income potential → Creates emotional buy-in
- Requires email → Captures lead
- Unique data → Can't be found elsewhere

### Principle
>
> Specificity lives in the calculator. Everything else frames the opportunity without giving away the specifics.

---

## 4. Content Philosophy

**Frame the opportunity. Don't solve the problem.**

### What We Do

- Help users understand what affects their outcome
- Create urgency around personalization
- Position our tools/services as the bridge

### What We Avoid

- Generic advice that sends them elsewhere
- Step-by-step instructions they can follow without us
- Content that satisfies curiosity without driving action

### The Test
>
> After reading this page, is the user more likely to use our calculator or to Google something else?

---

## 5. Funnel Protection

**Paid execution must remain valuable.**

### Tiers and Their Boundaries

| Tier | What's Included | What's Protected |
|------|-----------------|------------------|
| Free | Income potential (calculator) | How to achieve it |
| Books (~$20) | Frameworks, mental models | Implementation details |
| Services ($500+) | Actual execution, done-with/for-you | — |
| Membership ($99/mo) | Ongoing optimization | — |

### Rule
>
> If free content makes paid content unnecessary, the funnel is broken.

---

## 6. Building for Conversion

**Every component should have a job in the funnel.**

### Questions to Ask

1. What action do I want the user to take after this?
2. Does this create forward momentum or satisfy curiosity?
3. Is this the right level of specificity for this tier?

### Component Design Principle
>
> CTAs should be explicit. Users should know exactly what happens when they click.

---

## Quick Reference

| Decision | Ask This |
|----------|----------|
| Adding content | Does it push toward calculator/offer? |
| Adding features | Which segment? What journey stage? |
| Adding specifics | Is this free tier or paid tier content? |
| Adding pages | Is this in the conversion flow or supporting it? |
